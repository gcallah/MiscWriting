## Addendum to the main voices document

Each of these communal voices has its own tendency towards excess.

The military community is always tempted by its capacity for effective violence to achieve domination over as
many other communities, whether they are inside or outside its current military control, as possible.

The political community has a ineradicable tendency to extend its rules into every aspect of citizens' lives, in
other words, towards totalitarianism.

The religious community's vice is its tendency towards dogmatism and fanaticism, in other words, claiming that it is the
only voice worth attending to.

The revolutionary community regularly by failing to ask the question whether overthrowing the existing order is actually
likely to make things better, instead destroying a flawed, but workable order, and leaving in its place something worse.

And the ecological community is always subject to the temptation to become absorbed in their communal practices, and
forget the fact that they are sheltered by forces outside community.
